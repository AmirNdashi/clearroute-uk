About This Service

A Biometric Residence Permit (BRP) is a physical card issued by the Home Office as proof of your right to live, work, or study in the UK. Since 2024, the UK Home Office has been transitioning from physical BRP cards to eVisas — a fully digital immigration status accessible online through the UKVI (UK Visas and Immigration) system.

ClearRoute UK provides step-by-step guidance through BRP collection, renewal, replacement, and the eVisa system — helping you understand your immigration status, access your digital right to stay, and resolve any issues that arise. We keep you informed at every step and handle follow-up correspondence where needed.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is the difference between a BRP and an eVisa?
A BRP is a physical card that acts as proof of your right to live, work, or study in the UK. An eVisa is the digital equivalent — your immigration status is stored online and accessed through a UKVI account. The UK Home Office has been transitioning from BRPs to eVisas since 2024.
My BRP has expired or is about to expire — what do I do?
If your leave to remain is still valid but your BRP has expired, you'll need to apply for a replacement. If both your BRP and leave are expiring, you'll need to apply for an extension of your visa/leave. We'll assess your situation and guide you through the correct process.
I can't access my eVisa account — can you help?
Yes — eVisa access issues are common. We guide you through the UKVI account recovery process and, where needed, help you report the issue to the Home Office and get it resolved as quickly as possible.
