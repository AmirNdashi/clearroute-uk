About This Service

Converting a foreign driving licence to a UK licence is a process governed entirely by the DVLA (Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency). The requirements vary significantly depending on your country of origin — some licences qualify for a direct exchange, while others require additional testing.

ClearRoute UK handles the entire process alongside you — from confirming your eligibility and the correct category of exchange, to preparing your documents, completing the D9 medical form where required, and submitting everything correctly to the DVLA. We have supported hundreds of clients from over 40 countries through this process and know exactly what each case requires.

How the Process Works

Step 1 — Free Consultation (Day 1)

Frequently Asked Questions
Can any foreign driving licence be converted to a UK licence?
Not all foreign licences are eligible for a direct exchange. Licences from certain countries (called "designated countries") can be exchanged without a test. Others require you to sit the UK theory and practical tests. We'll confirm your eligibility during the free consultation.
Do I need to retake my driving test?
It depends on your country of origin. If your country has a reciprocal licence exchange agreement with the UK, you won't need to retake any tests. If not, you'll need to pass both the theory and practical tests. We'll advise you specifically based on your licence.
How long can I drive in the UK on my foreign licence?
If you're a new UK resident, you can generally drive on your foreign licence for up to 12 months from the date you became a UK resident. After that, you'll need a UK licence. We recommend starting the conversion process well within this window.
What are the DVLA government fees — are they included?
DVLA government fees are paid directly to the DVLA and are separate from our consultancy fee. We'll outline all expected costs clearly during your consultation so there are no surprises.
