How to Convert Your Foreign Driving Licence in the UK

A complete, step-by-step guide covering eligibility, required documents, the D1 application process, costs, and timeline expectations.

July 21, 2026 8 min read Driving Licence

If you have recently moved to the United Kingdom and hold a driving licence issued by another country, you may be wondering whether you can exchange it for a full UK driving licence. The process is governed by the DVLA (Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency), and the requirements differ significantly depending on where your licence was issued.

This guide covers everything you need to know — from eligibility and required documents to costs, timelines, and how ClearRoute UK's driving licence conversion service can help you through every step.

Can You Exchange Your Foreign Driving Licence?

The first and most important question is whether your country qualifies for a direct licence exchange. The DVLA divides countries into two categories:

Designated (Exchangeable) Countries

If your licence was issued by a designated country, you can exchange it for a UK licence without taking any further driving tests. Designated countries include most EU and EEA member states, Australia, Barbados, British Virgin Islands, Canada, Falkland Islands, Faroe Islands, Gibraltar, Hong Kong, Japan, Monaco, New Zealand, Republic of Korea, Singapore, South Africa, Switzerland, Taiwan, and Zimbabwe.

Non-Designated (Non-Exchangeable) Countries

If your licence was issued by a country not on the designated list, you cannot directly exchange it. Instead, you will need to apply for a provisional UK licence and pass both the theory test and practical driving test. However, you may still be able to use your foreign licence to drive in the UK for up to 12 months from becoming resident.

Documents Required for Licence Conversion

Whether you are exchanging a designated licence or applying as a new driver, you will need to provide several documents. The exact list varies by case, but the typical requirements include:

  • Current foreign driving licence — the original physical licence card
  • Valid passport or national identity card (for EEA applicants)
  • Proof of UK address — recent utility bill, bank statement, or tenancy agreement (dated within the last 3 months)
  • Passport-style photograph — meeting DVLA photo standards
  • Completed D1 application form — the official DVLA form for licence applications
  • D9 medical form — required if you have certain medical conditions or are over a certain age
  • Official translation — if your licence is not in English, you will need a certified translation from a recognised translator
  • DVLA fee payment — the government fee for processing the application

It is critical to get these documents right the first time. Incorrect or incomplete applications are a leading cause of delays and rejections. ClearRoute UK provides a personalised document checklist and reviews every item before submission.

The D1 Application Form Process

The D1 form is the cornerstone of the driving licence conversion process. Here is how to complete it correctly:

  1. Obtain the form: You can download the D1 form from the GOV.UK website or request a paper copy by post from the DVLA.
  2. Section 1 — Personal Details: Fill in your full name, date of birth, address, and contact information exactly as they appear on your passport.
  3. Section 2 — Licence Details: Provide information about your current foreign driving licence, including the issuing country and licence number.
  4. Section 3 — Medical Declaration: Disclose any medical conditions that may affect your driving. Certain conditions require a D9 medical form to be completed by your GP.
  5. Section 4 — Declaration: Sign and date the form to confirm all information is accurate.
  6. Submit with supporting documents: Send the completed D1 form, your foreign licence, passport, proof of address, photo, and fee to the DVLA at the address specified on the form.
Common mistake: Many applicants rush through Section 3 (medical) and fail to disclose conditions or provide incorrect information. This can lead to delays of several months or even a rejection. ClearRoute UK checks every section of your form against DVLA requirements.

Costs Involved

The costs for converting your driving licence include both government fees and any consultancy support you choose:

  • DVLA application fee: £43 for a standard licence exchange (as of 2026)
  • D9 medical form fee: Varies — typically £30–£100 depending on your GP surgery
  • Official translation: Approximately £20–£50 if required
  • Photograph: £6–£15 if taken at a booth or pharmacy

These are separate from ClearRoute UK's consultancy fee, which covers eligibility assessment, document preparation and review, form completion guidance, submission support, and progress tracking.

Timeline Expectations

The entire process from consultation to receiving your UK licence typically takes between 2 and 8 weeks:

  • Eligibility assessment: Same day (during your free consultation)
  • Document preparation: 2–7 days
  • DVLA processing: 1–6 weeks from receipt of application
  • Delivery: 3–5 working days after approval

Processing times at the DVLA can vary significantly depending on the time of year and current application volumes. ClearRoute UK monitors your application and handles any follow-up correspondence to keep things moving.

What Happens After Approval?

Once the DVLA approves your application, your new UK driving licence will be posted to your registered address. It is a photocard licence valid until your 70th birthday (renewed every 10 years thereafter). You can then use it to drive any vehicle in the categories covered by your licence throughout the UK and EU.
