How to Get a National Insurance Number as a Foreign National in the UK

A complete guide to applying for a National Insurance number — eligibility, required documents, the HMRC application process, and how long it takes.

July 21, 2026 7 min read NI Number

If you have recently moved to the United Kingdom and plan to work, you will need a National Insurance (NI) number. This unique reference number is used by HMRC to track your tax contributions, National Insurance contributions, and entitlement to benefits and public services.

For foreign nationals, the process of obtaining an NI number has changed significantly in recent years. This guide explains everything you need to know, and how ClearRoute UK's NI Number Application service can help you get your number quickly and correctly.

What Is a National Insurance Number?

A National Insurance number is a unique alphanumeric identifier (format: QQ 12 34 56 A) assigned to every eligible UK resident. It ensures that the tax and National Insurance contributions you pay are correctly recorded against your name. Without an NI number, your employer cannot properly report your earnings to HMRC, and you may end up paying emergency tax.

Beyond employment, your NI number is also used for:

  • Claiming State Pension and other benefits
  • Accessing certain public services
  • Verifying your identity for financial and government purposes
  • Registering with a GP or accessing the NHS

Who Is Eligible for an NI Number?

You are eligible for a National Insurance number if you:

  • Are lawfully resident in the United Kingdom
  • Have the right to work in the UK (either through settled status, a valid work visa, or UK citizenship)
  • Intend to work, are already working, or are claiming benefits

Foreign nationals who have a Biometric Residence Permit (BRP) or an eVisa with the right to work are typically eligible. If you are unsure about your immigration status, you may need to check your BRP or eVisa details before applying.

Note: If you already have an NI number from a previous stay in the UK, you do not need to apply again. Contact HMRC to request a replacement confirmation letter if you have lost your NI number.

Documents Required for an NI Number Application

To apply for an NI number, you will need to provide documents that prove your identity and your right to work in the UK. The standard requirements include:

  • Valid passport — from your country of citizenship
  • Biometric Residence Permit (BRP) — if you have one, this confirms your immigration status and right to work
  • Proof of UK address — such as a utility bill, bank statement, or tenancy agreement dated within the last 3 months
  • Employment details — if you already have a job offer or are working, you may need a letter from your employer confirming your employment

If you do not have a BRP and hold an eVisa, you can use your eVisa share code to prove your immigration status. ClearRoute UK provides guidance on BRP and eVisa documentation.

How to Apply for an NI Number

As of 2026, the process for applying for a National Insurance number has moved primarily online. Here is the step-by-step process:

Step 1 — Check Your Eligibility Online

Visit the GOV.UK website and use the eligibility checker to confirm you qualify for an NI number. You will be asked about your residency status, right to work, and whether you already have an NI number.

Step 2 — Complete the Online Application

If eligible, you will be directed to complete an online application form. This asks for your personal details, contact information, and immigration status. You will also need to upload scanned copies or photos of your identity documents.

Step 3 — Attend an Identity Verification Interview (If Required)

In some cases, HMRC may require you to attend an identity verification interview — either in person at a Jobcentre Plus office or via a video call. This is more common for applicants whose documents cannot be verified digitally.

Step 4 — Wait for HMRC Processing

Once your application and documents are submitted, HMRC processes your application and issues your NI number. If approved, you will receive a letter containing your NI number by post within 4–6 weeks.

Step 5 — Receive Your NI Number Letter

The NI number letter is an official HMRC document. Keep it safe — you will need to provide it to your employer, pension provider, and when claiming benefits. You can also access your NI number online through your personal tax account once it is set up.

Tip: The most common reason for NI number application delays is incorrect or insufficient documentation. ClearRoute UK reviews every document before submission to ensure it meets HMRC's requirements.

How Long Does It Take?

The timeline for receiving your NI number varies depending on the application method:

  • Online application: 4–6 weeks from submission
  • Application with interview: 6–10 weeks (including interview scheduling and processing)
  • Replacement NI number letter: 2–3 weeks

Processing times can be longer during peak periods, such as the start of the tax year (April) or the autumn intake of international students and workers.

What to Do While Waiting

If you start a job before receiving your NI number, do not worry. Your employer can use a temporary NI number (usually a code like "TN 00 00 00 A") to process your payroll. Once your permanent NI number arrives, provide it to your employer so they can update their records and ensure your tax contributions are correctly allocated.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Applying when you already have an NI number: Check whether you were previously assigned an NI number (e.g., during a prior stay in the UK). Applying again will cause confusion and delays.
  • Using incorrect address proof: Your proof of address must be recent (within 3 months) and show your full name and current UK address. HMRC is strict about this.
  • Submitting unclear document scans: HMRC requires clear, legible copies. Blurry or incomplete scans will be rejected.
  • Not checking your right to work first: If your visa or immigration status does not permit work, your application will be refused. Check your BRP or eVisa before applying.
