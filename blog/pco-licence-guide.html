PCO Licence Application: Complete Step-by-Step Guide for 2026

Everything you need to know about getting your PCO licence in London — requirements, medical, DBS check, topographical test, and the full application process.

July 21, 2026 9 min read PCO Licence

The Private Hire (PCO) licence is essential for anyone who wants to work as a minicab or private hire driver in London. Regulated by Transport for London (TfL), the application process involves multiple steps, several different checks, and can take weeks to complete.

This guide breaks down everything you need to know about getting your PCO licence in 2026, and how ClearRoute UK's PCO Licence service can help you navigate the process smoothly.

What Is a PCO Licence?

A PCO (Public Carriage Office) licence, officially called a Private Hire Licence, is issued by Transport for London (TfL). It authorises you to operate a private hire vehicle — such as a minicab or a pre-booked car — in London. You do not need a PCO licence to drive a black cab (those require a separate Hackney Carriage licence).

Unlike black cabs, private hire vehicles cannot be hailed on the street. They must be pre-booked through a licensed operator such as Uber, Bolt, Addison Lee, or a local minicab firm.

Who Needs a PCO Licence?

You need a PCO licence if you want to work as a private hire driver in London. This applies whether you plan to drive for a ride-hailing app like Uber or Bolt, or for a traditional minicab operator. Without a valid PCO licence, you cannot legally accept and fulfil private hire bookings in the capital.

PCO Licence Requirements

To be eligible for a PCO licence, you must meet the following requirements set by TfL:

  • Full UK driving licence — you must hold a full UK (or Northern Ireland) driving licence. If you have a foreign licence, you will need to convert it to a UK licence first.
  • Right to work in the UK — you must have the legal right to work as a private hire driver. This typically means holding UK citizenship, settled status, or a valid work visa that permits self-employed driving work.
  • Enhanced DBS check — TfL requires an enhanced Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) check. This checks for criminal convictions, cautions, and reprimands.
  • Medical fitness certificate — you must meet the DVLA Group 2 medical standards (the same standards required for bus and lorry drivers). A doctor must complete a D4 medical form.
  • Topographical skills assessment — you must pass the TfL topographical assessment, which tests your ability to navigate London without relying on satellite navigation.
  • PCO theory test — you must pass the PCO-specific theory test, which covers the Highway Code, driving theory, and private hire regulations.
  • Right-to-work check — TfL will verify your immigration status to confirm you are eligible to work as a private hire driver.
Important: From 2025, TfL requires all new applicants to pass an updated safeguarding assessment. This is part of TfL's ongoing commitment to passenger safety. ClearRoute UK includes safeguarding preparation in our PCO support package.

Step-by-Step PCO Application Process

Step 1 — Get a Full UK Driving Licence

If you hold a foreign driving licence, you must first exchange it for a full UK licence through the DVLA. This is a prerequisite for any PCO application. ClearRoute UK can help with your licence conversion.

Step 2 — Complete Your Medical (D4 Form)

You need to visit a doctor to complete the DVLA D4 medical form. The doctor will check your vision, cardiovascular health, neurological function, and general fitness to drive. The medical must be dated within the last 4 months when you submit your application.

Step 3 — Apply for Your Enhanced DBS Check

TfL will arrange an enhanced DBS check through the GOV.UK Update Service. You will need to provide proof of identity and address. The DBS check typically takes 2–4 weeks, but can take longer if there are complications.

Step 4 — Book and Pass the PCO Theory Test

The PCO theory test consists of two parts: multiple-choice questions on the Highway Code and driving theory, and a hazard perception test. You can book this through the DVSA website. Study materials are available from the DVSA and through third-party apps.

Step 5 — Pass the Topographical Assessment

The topographical assessment tests your knowledge of London's geography. You will be asked to identify locations, plan routes, and demonstrate that you can navigate the city without a sat-nav. TfL offers official study materials to help you prepare.

Step 6 — Submit Your PCO Application to TfL

Once you have all the required documents and test results, you can submit your full PCO application to TfL through the TfL licensing portal. The application fee is payable at this stage.

Step 7 — Wait for TfL Processing

TfL reviews your application, verifies your documents, and conducts the right-to-work check. Processing typically takes 15–20 working days, but can vary depending on application volumes and the complexity of your case.

Costs Involved

The total cost of getting a PCO licence can vary, but here are the expected fees:

  • PCO application fee (TfL): Approximately £325 (for a 3-year licence)
  • D4 medical form: £50–£120 depending on your GP
  • Enhanced DBS check: £50–£60
  • PCO theory test: £23 (car) or £23 (private hire)
  • Topographical assessment: £18
  • Driving licence conversion (if needed): £43 DVLA fee + consultancy fee

Timeline: How Long Does It Take?

A typical PCO licence application takes 15–20 working days for TfL to process once all documents are submitted. However, the entire process — from getting your UK driving licence to passing tests and submitting — can take 4–12 weeks depending on your circumstances.

  • Licence conversion (if needed): 2–8 weeks
  • Medical appointment: 1–2 weeks (depending on GP availability)
  • DBS check: 2–4 weeks
  • PCO theory test: book within 1–2 weeks, study time varies
  • Topographical assessment: book within 1–2 weeks
  • TfL processing: 15–20 working days

Tips for a Successful PCO Application

  • Start early: The biggest mistake applicants make is leaving everything to the last minute. Begin the process at least 3 months before you need to start driving.
  • Get your documents right: Incomplete or incorrect documents are the most common cause of delays. Have everything checked before submission.
  • Prepare for the topographical test: Many applicants underestimate this test. Study London's geography thoroughly using TfL's official materials.
  • Keep your DBS certificate accessible: Register for the DBS Update Service so TfL can verify your status at any time.
